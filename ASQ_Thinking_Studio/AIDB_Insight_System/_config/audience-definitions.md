# audience-definitions.md

Who gets which card and why.

Use this file in Stage 03 to match insight objects to the right sender-recipient pair and channel.

---

## How to use this file

An insight object has a claim type, a topic, and a channel fit score. Match those against the audience profiles below to identify the right card format and the right sender-recipient pair.

The sender is an AIDB listener. The recipient is someone the sender would forward something to. The card reduces the friction of that forward — it gives the sender the context they would otherwise have to write themselves.

---

## Audience profiles

### The Enterprise AI Champion
**Who they are:** Someone inside a large organization who is responsible for or deeply interested in AI adoption. May be in IT, L&D, strategy, or a business unit that has deployed AI tools.
**What they share:** Insights that help them make the case internally, reframe the conversation, or give their team useful language.
**Who they share with:** Their team, their manager, peer leaders in other departments, internal Slack channels.
**Best card format:** Team Prompt
**Best moment types:** Adoption reframes, capability distinctions, usage behavior insights
**Example trigger:** "My team is using AI but I'm not sure they're using it well — this reframes it in a way I could share in our next all-hands."

---

### The Founder or Operator
**Who they are:** Building something, or running something, in or adjacent to the AI space. High signal-to-noise ratio as an audience — they do not have time for hype.
**What they share:** Insights that reframe a decision they are actively making or a market they are watching.
**Who they share with:** Co-founders, investors, advisors, peer operators in a group chat or Slack.
**Best card format:** Reality Check or Product Question
**Best moment types:** Market structure insights, monetization logic, contrarian takes on obvious narratives
**Example trigger:** "We were about to make an assumption about agentic commerce — this reframes the question in a way I need to bring to my team before we ship."

---

### The Investor or Analyst
**Who they are:** Watching the AI landscape professionally. Needs to have an informed, defensible position on where value is accruing.
**What they share:** Insights that sharpen the question or reframe the conventional wisdom in their portfolio or coverage area.
**Who they share with:** Partners, portfolio companies, research colleagues, Substack or newsletter audience.
**Best card format:** Reality Check
**Best moment types:** Economic logic, market structure, where conventional narratives are wrong
**Example trigger:** "Every headline said X. This episode explains why the real story is Y. I'm sending this to my whole portfolio."

---

### The Thoughtful Professional
**Who they are:** Smart, curious, uses AI in their work but is not building AI products. Could be a marketer, consultant, lawyer, designer, writer, or any knowledge worker paying attention to AI.
**What they share:** Insights that give them something interesting to bring to a conversation or that help them think through something they are experiencing at work.
**Who they share with:** Colleagues, professional peers, people in their industry Slack or network.
**Best card format:** Team Prompt or Reality Check
**Best moment types:** Usage behavior, adoption patterns, things that reframe how AI fits into their daily work
**Example trigger:** "I've been trying to explain this to my colleagues for weeks — this says it better than I could."

---

### The Company Champion
**Who they are:** An AIDB listener who is actively bringing AIDB content into their organization — forwarding episodes, starting discussions, running internal AI education.
**What they share:** Anything that makes it easier to run an internal discussion, training, or briefing.
**Who they share with:** Their team, their Slack, their internal newsletter, their manager.
**Best card format:** Team Prompt (most structured for internal use)
**Best moment types:** Reframes that open a team discussion, usage behavior insights, adoption distinctions
**Special note:** Company Champions are the highest-signal distribution path AIDB has. Cards designed for Company Champions should be optimized for internal professional use, not external social sharing.

## Channel guide

| Channel | Best format | Optimal length | Tone |
|---------|------------|----------------|------|
| Slack (team) | Team Prompt | Short — scannable in 30 seconds | Collegial, direct |
| Email (professional) | Reality Check or Briefing | Medium — readable in 90 seconds | Slightly more formal |
| LinkedIn | Reality Check | Short — hook in first line | Public-facing, confident |
| Team meeting | Team Prompt | Discussion question format | Conversational |
| Client deck or briefing | Product Question or Reality Check | Tight — one idea only | Professional, grounded |

---

## The sender test

Before finalizing a card, ask: would a real AIDB listener actually send this to the person we named?

If the answer is "maybe" or "I'm not sure," the card is not specific enough. Make the sender and recipient more concrete and rewrite.

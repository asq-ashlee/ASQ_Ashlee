# aidb-brand-constraints.md

What the AIDB Insight System must never do.

These constraints apply to every stage, every card, every insight object, every macro-story arc. They are not guidelines. They are hard stops.

---

## The brand in one sentence

AIDB earns trust by being rigorous, fast, and editorially honest. Growth that erodes any of those three is not growth.

---

## Hard stops — never do these

**Never flatten NLW's judgment into a soundbite.**
The value of AIDB is not the information. It is the editorial layer — the framing, the skepticism, the context Nathan brings. Any card or object that strips that layer and presents the raw claim as if it were a standalone fact has failed.

**Never present an interpretation as a fact.**
Every insight object has a claim type field for this reason. A card that presents NLW's interpretation as established truth misrepresents both the content and the host.

**Never use fear hooks, "this changes everything" framing, or engagement bait.**
AIDB does not grow by alarming people. It grows by being useful to people who are already paying attention.

**Never produce shallow quote cards.**
A quote without context is not an insight object. It is a fragment. Fragments do not carry the trust that makes AIDB worth sharing.

**Never over-automate in ways that flatten the editorial voice.**
Summaries that could have been written by any AI about any podcast are not AIDB summaries. Every output should be unmistakably grounded in this episode, this argument, this moment.

**Never connect insight objects across episodes without a compatibility check.**
Cross-episode connections that violate time, claim type, confidence, speaker intent, or scope boundary damage the integrity of the macro-story layer. A false arc is worse than no arc.

**Never prioritize reach over trust density.**
Wrong audience acquired fast is worse than right audience grown slowly.

---

## Brand check questions (run before any card ships)

1. Does this card represent NLW's actual position, or a simplified version of it?
2. Is the claim type accurately represented — fact, interpretation, prediction, or opinion?
3. Could someone forward this and damage AIDB's reputation if the recipient checked the source?
4. Does this feel like something a thoughtful AIDB listener would be proud to send?
5. Does this reduce or increase trust in AIDB as a source?

If any answer raises doubt, do not ship. Revise or drop.

---

## The taste test

Would NLW be comfortable with how this card represents his work?

If the answer is anything other than a clear yes, the card does not ship.

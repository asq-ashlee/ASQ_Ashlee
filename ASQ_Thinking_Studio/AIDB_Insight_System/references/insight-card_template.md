# Card Formats

Three formats. Each one is designed for a specific channel and a specific sender-recipient relationship.
Choose the format based on who is sending it and where it lands — not based on the content alone.

---

## Format 1: Reality Check
**Channel:** Slack, email, group chat
**Sender:** Anyone who follows AI news and has colleagues who don't
**Recipient:** A team, a peer group, or a professional channel
**Use when:** The moment pushes back on a lazy narrative or reframes a story the recipient has already heard

```
AIDB Reality Check — [Episode Title]

The moment:
[2-3 lines. The story as everyone heard it. Then the actual story.]

NLW's point:
[1-2 lines. The reframe. What this means for how you read the next headline.]

Worth the 20 minutes:
[link]
```

---

## Format 2: Product Question
**Channel:** Team Slack, internal discussion, product review, design critique
**Sender:** A product person, designer, strategist, or founder
**Recipient:** A team making decisions about a product, feature, or market
**Use when:** The moment surfaces an assumption worth questioning before a decision gets made

```
AIDB Product Question — [Episode Title]

The distinction:
[2-3 lines. The thing most people assume. The thing that complicates it.]

The question nobody is asking:
[1-2 lines. The sharper version of the question your team should be asking.]

[One closing line that lands the human stakes.]

[link]
```

---

## Format 3: Team Prompt
**Channel:** Slack, team meeting, L&D, internal newsletter
**Sender:** A manager, team lead, AI champion, or L&D person
**Recipient:** A team that has AI access but hasn't fully changed how they work
**Use when:** The moment gives language to something the team is experiencing but hasn't named

```
AIDB Team Prompt — [Episode Title]

Key idea:
[2-3 lines. The insight, stated plainly.]

Why it matters:
[1-2 lines. What this means for how your team is working right now.]

Team question:
[One discussion question. Open-ended. Brings the idea into the room.]

[link]
```

---

## Usage notes

- Every card ends with the episode link. Always. Attribution travels with the card.
- Cards are not summaries. They carry one moment, not the full episode.
- The format is a starting point. Adjust tone for the audience. A card going to a VC group chat sounds different than one going to a learning and development channel.
- When in doubt, use Format 1. It has the broadest range and the lowest barrier to send.

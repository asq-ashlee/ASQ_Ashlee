FILE 06 — WORKFLOW SYSTEM

Core Workflow

1. Artwork created (human)
2. Artwork interpreted (AI)
3. Product options generated (AI + rules)
4. Listing generated (AI)
5. Content generated (AI)
6. Human review
7. Publish

AI Role

AI assists in:

* description writing
* title formatting
* tag generation
* product mapping

Human Role

Human is responsible for:

* taste
* final voice alignment
* artwork itself

## Review State Requirements

All AI-generated operational drafts must clearly separate:

1. AI Draft
2. Owner-Reviewed Corrections
3. Approved Operational Memory

AI Draft may include:
- generated interpretation
- title suggestions
- metadata suggestions
- mood/color/light observations
- product opportunity ideas
- content opportunity ideas

Owner-Reviewed Corrections must include:
- confirmed facts
- rejected AI assumptions
- corrected language
- owner-approved emotional positioning
- owner-approved workflow status

Approved Operational Memory must include only:
- owner-confirmed artwork facts
- owner-approved title
- owner-approved description or summary
- confirmed metadata
- confirmed product/channel relationships
- approved workflow status

AI drafts should never be treated as operational memory until the owner has reviewed and approved them.

## Notion Dataset Placement

Final approved operational content may be entered into one of the three Notion datasets:

- Art Pieces
- Products
- Content

Use these datasets only for owner-reviewed, approved records.

Drafts, AI suggestions, unconfirmed interpretations, and unapproved product ideas should remain in workspace draft files until reviewed.

Dataset routing:

- Approved artwork-level memory → Art Pieces
- Approved product-level records → Products
- Approved audience-facing content → Content

The Notion datasets are structured operational records. They should not become a dumping ground for raw AI drafts.

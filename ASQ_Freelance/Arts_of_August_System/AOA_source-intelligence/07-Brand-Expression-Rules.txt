FILE 07 — BRAND EXPRESSION RULES

Titles

* Must feel like a moment
* Must anchor to time, place, or condition
* Titles should avoid purely literal object description without emotional or atmospheric framing.

Descriptions

* Begin with atmosphere
* Stay observational
* Avoid narrative

Language

* Light is the emotional carrier
* Color expresses tone
* Stillness is the default state

Boundaries

Do NOT:

* use generic décor language
* over-explain
* shift into storytelling
* prioritize SEO over voice

System Separation

* Expression = title + description
* Discovery = tags

## Grounded Emotional Language

Arts of August writing should prioritize grounded emotional observation over lyrical abstraction.

Descriptions should feel:
- visually observant
- emotionally calm
- human
- atmospheric
- restrained
- believable

Prioritize:
- emotional resonance
- visual observation
- atmosphere
- human storytelling
- emotional specificity
- sensory grounding

Prefer:
- direct observation
- quiet atmosphere
- visual realism
- calm emotional tone

Avoid:
- generic ecommerce filler language
- exaggerated poetic metaphors
- dramatic emotional claims
- overly romantic phrasing
- keyword stuffing
- conversion-focused urgency
- corporate marketing tone
- “the sky held its breath” style language
- excessive manufacturing/shipping detail unless operationally necessary

The goal is:
quiet emotional resonance, not performative poetry.

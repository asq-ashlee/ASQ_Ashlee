## Layered Listing Construction

Arts of August listings combine information from multiple operational systems.

Examples:
- Product specifications → Printful or artist records
- Shipping logic → Etsy shipping profiles or Printful systems
- Emotional positioning → AI + human review
- Product imagery → artist photography, Printful mockups, Canva assets

AI may assist with:
- listing structure
- emotional storytelling
- product positioning
- title exploration
- readability improvements

AI should not invent operational product details that belong to authoritative systems.

## Title System — Repeatable Formula

Every artwork title follows a two-tier structure.

Tier 1 — Site display title (lyrical, short)
Tier 2 — Etsy listing title (SEO-extended)

### Four-filter test for Tier 1 title

Before approving a title, it must pass all four filters:

1. Literal keyword — does it contain or suggest a word a buyer would actually search?
2. Emotionally open — does it leave room for the buyer's own meaning without forcing a specific interpretation?
3. Catalog voice match — does it feel consistent with existing Arts of August titles (short, sensory, non-literal-locational)?
4. Keyword tail compatible — does it pair cleanly with an SEO clarifier for the Etsy title?

### Title construction process

Step 1 — Identify the hook
The hook is one emotional focal moment in the painting — not the whole scene.
Example: in a seascape with clouds, the hook may be the light breaking through, not the bay or the trees.

Step 2 — Name the hook (Tier 1)
Short. Lyrical. Sensory. Usually 3–5 words.
Examples: "Light Through the Clouds", "The Violet Hour", "Where the Water Wanders"

Step 3 — Extend for Etsy (Tier 2)
Format: [Tier 1 title] — [literal product description], [location keyword], [subject keywords], [format keyword]
Example: "Light Through the Clouds — Original Oil Painting, Coastal Sunset Sky, Maine Seascape Wall Art, Signed by Artist"

### What to avoid in Tier 1 titles

* Literal location names unless essential to the emotional hook (e.g. "Over Penobscot Bay" narrows unnecessarily)
* Religious or spiritually specific language (e.g. "Heaven's Window") — narrows the buyer pool
* Generic descriptors (e.g. "Beautiful Sunset") — does not pass catalog voice filter
* Over-long phrases — Tier 1 title should be memorable and speakable

## GEO / AI Search Rules

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is how Arts of August content surfaces in AI-powered search results (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) when buyers search in natural language.

AI search engines read alt text, descriptions, and structured data differently from keyword-based search. They favor complete, natural-language sentences that describe the image accurately.

### Alt text formula

Every artwork must have a GEO-optimized alt text field.

Format:
[Title] — [medium] of [literal visual description including subject, colors, lighting, place] by [artist full name and location].

Example:
"Light Through the Clouds — original oil painting of sun rays breaking through lavender and peach clouds over a calm Maine coastal bay at sunset, by Maine artist Kaleigh Anderson."

Rules:
* Always include medium (oil on canvas, framed fine art print, canvas print)
* Always include a literal visual description — describe what is physically in the image
* Always include artist full name
* Always include location (Maine, Maine coast, Scarborough Maine)
* Never use the story or emotional hook language here — this field is literal and descriptive
* Never use vague descriptors like "beautiful" or "stunning"

### Alt text vs Story field

These are two different fields with two different jobs.

| Field | Audience | Language | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| Story | Human shoppers | Lyrical, emotional, one sentence | Emotional hook — why this painting exists, what it feels like |
| Alt text | Screen readers + AI search | Literal, descriptive, one sentence | Describe what is visually in the image + artist + place |

Never use story language in alt text. Never use alt text language in story.

### Natural language search phrases to target

When writing descriptions and alt text, consider that buyers search in natural language:
* "oil painting of sun through clouds over water"
* "Maine coastal art for living room"
* "original painting of evening light on the ocean"
* "framed coastal wall art Maine artist"
* "atmospheric seascape painting"

The alt text and description should contain the words and phrases that appear naturally in these queries.

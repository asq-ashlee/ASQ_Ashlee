A physical product still runs on information. The box was tangible, but the real product was the system that chose, coordinated, timed, budgeted, designed, and delivered everything again and again.

Case Study 01 — Turning Email Marketing Into a System
Analyzed enterprise email marketing workflows and identified the gap between active execution and a system that could carry learning forward.

Core idea:
What looked like execution complexity was really structure absence.

Case Study 02 — When AI Agents Hit Reality
Explored agent-style workflows connected to structured content and identified practical limits around access, scale, memory, state, and orchestration.

Core idea:
AI works best as part of a system, not as the system itself.

Case Study 03 — Designing Workflows That Can Actually Think
Reframed workflows as loops with inputs, structure, decisions, support, outputs, and feedback.

Core idea:
The goal is not just to use AI inside workflows. The goal is to design workflows AI can actually operate inside.

Case Study 04 — Event Systems
Translated event planning experience into real-time systems work: vendors, timelines, themes, people, dependencies, and live execution.

Core idea:
A system that works once is execution. A system that works repeatedly under pressure is design.

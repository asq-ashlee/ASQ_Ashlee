## Job Evaluation Rubric

### Important Scoring Guidance

Do not penalize practical, lower-risk roles too heavily just because they are not perfect long-term ASQ Ashlee alignment.

If a role strongly matches Ashlee’s proven experience, offers remote/flexible work, and could provide stable income or a credible next step, it should generally score 8+ even if it is not her ideal future direction.

Use the Opportunity Type to separate practical fit from strategic fit.

For example:
- A strong SFMC/lifecycle role may be Low Hanging Fruit or Core Fit even if it is execution-heavy.
- A more future-aligned AI/systems role may be Strategic or North Star even if it has bigger technical gaps.

Recommendation language:
- “Apply” can mean worth applying without over-investing.
- “Maybe” should be reserved for roles where fit, energy, or requirements are genuinely uncertain.

---

### Step 1: Understand the role

- What is this role actually asking for?
- What problem are they trying to solve?
- What signals are clear vs vague?

---

### Step 2: Fit Scoring (1–10)

Evaluate:

- Skills match
- Flexibility
- AI/system relevance
- Ability to stand out
- Alignment with long-term direction

---

### Step 3: Thinking Quality Test

- Does this role value thinking or just execution?
- Are decisions visible or opaque?
- Can systems be improved?

---

### Step 4: Technical Stretch Flag

If a role requires technical ownership beyond my current proof, flag it clearly.

Examples:
- API implementation
- production engineering
- advanced SQL ownership
- data pipelines
- ML lifecycle
- platform administration as a primary responsibility

Do not automatically skip these roles.

Classify as:
- Reasonable stretch
- Significant stretch
- Misaligned technical requirement

Then explain whether the role is still worth applying to.

---

### Step 5: Opportunity Type

- Core fit
- Low hanging fruit
- Strategic
- Experimental
- North Star
- Skip

North Star means: not an immediate application priority, but useful as a model for future positioning, skill development, or ASQ Ashlee content.

If a role is conceptually aligned but not currently realistic due to seniority, required title history, missing domain experience, or technical ownership gaps, mark it as North Star instead of Skip.

---

### Step 6: Output

For each role:

- Fit score
- Opportunity type
- Technical stretch flag, if relevant
- Why it fits
- Concerns
- Best positioning angle
- Recommended resume version
- Case study to use
- Recommendation: apply / maybe / save / skip
- Next step

---

### Rules

- Do not evaluate more than 3–5 roles at once
- Prioritize depth over volume
- Show thinking before conclusions
- Protect energy
- Do not recommend “maybe just in case” applications

---

### Language Rules

- Keep it simple
- Avoid jargon
- Prioritize clarity over cleverness

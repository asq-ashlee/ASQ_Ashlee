# First 30 Days — Experiment Design

This is a designed experiment, not a strategy deck.
It is meant to run, produce real signal, and build from there.

---

## Hypothesis

AIDB listeners are willing to share episode moments with colleagues and teams — but the friction of composing context stops them. A pre-packaged card removes that friction. If the card is specific enough, useful enough, and formatted for the right channel, sharing rates will increase and the ideas will travel further than episode links alone.

---

## Phase 1: Listen and map (Days 1–7)

Before building anything, understand how sharing already happens.

**Actions:**
- Talk to 3–5 existing AIDB listeners, especially any Company Champions
- Ask: what have you shared from AIDB in the last month? What did you share it in? What context did you add?
- Look at existing episode social posts: what format gets the most engagement? What gets forwarded vs liked?
- Read the weekly briefing as a sharer, not just a reader: what would you paste into Slack from this?

**Output:** A short map of existing share behaviors — what travels, where, and what friction exists.

---

## Phase 2: Score and select (Days 8–14)

Pick two recent episodes. Run every significant moment through the shareability rubric. Score fast — this is judgment practice, not analysis paralysis.

**Actions:**
- Identify 3–5 moments per episode
- Run each through the five rubric criteria
- Select the one highest-scoring moment per episode
- Note which card format fits each one and why

**Output:** Two moments selected for packaging, with rubric scores documented.

---

## Phase 3: Build and send (Days 15–21)

Build the two cards. Send them to real people in real channels.

**Actions:**
- Write each card using the appropriate format template
- Send to 5–8 real people — not a survey, actual sends in actual channels
- Recipients should be: existing AIDB listeners, Company Champions if accessible, professional peers who match the target audience for each card
- Send with one line of context: "Testing whether this format makes AIDB easier to share at work. Would you send this to anyone?"

**Output:** Two cards sent, initial reactions collected.

---

## Phase 4: Measure and learn (Days 22–30)

Track what you can. Ask what you can't track.

**What to track:**
- Click-through on episode links in the cards
- Forwards or reshares (email forward rate, Slack reposts)
- Direct replies to the send

**What to ask directly:**
- Did you share this with anyone?
- If yes: who and where?
- If no: what would have made you more likely to?

**The signal that matters most:**
"I sent this to my team" or "we talked about this in a meeting."
That means the idea traveled somewhere decisions happen.
That is the result worth designing toward.

**Output:** A one-page findings doc — what worked, what didn't, what the next experiment should test.

---

## What success looks like at Day 30

Not a dashboard. Not a system. One thing:

A card that at least two people forwarded without being asked to.

That proves the format works. Everything else builds from there.

---

## What this experiment does not answer

- Whether this works at scale (that's experiment 2)
- Whether clips or social posts outperform cards (that's experiment 3)
- Whether Company Champions need different formats than individual listeners (that's experiment 4)

One experiment at a time. Start here.

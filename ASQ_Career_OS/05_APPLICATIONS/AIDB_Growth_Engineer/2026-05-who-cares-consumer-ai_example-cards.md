# Example Cards — Who Cares About Consumer AI

Episode: Who Cares About Consumer AI
Link: https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/who-cares-about-consumer-ai/id1680633614?i=1000766507145

These are two finished cards built from this episode using the rubric and card format templates.
They are ready to use as reference for future episodes.

---

## Card 1: AI as a Convenient Alibi
Format used: Reality Check
Rubric scores: Specificity ✓ Novelty ✓ Actionability ✓ Trust density ✓ Channel fit ✓ (Slack, email, group chat)
Target sender: Strategist, analyst, comms person, anyone reading tech layoff news
Target recipient: A team discussing what AI is and isn't actually doing to headcount

```
AIDB Reality Check — Who Cares About Consumer AI

The moment:
Coinbase announced layoffs. Every headline called it AI-driven.
What got buried: Robinhood reported a 47% drop in crypto
trading revenue the week before.
Coinbase is a crypto company in a down market.
AI is a better story than that.

NLW's point:
AI will absolutely change employment. But letting it become
a convenient alibi makes it harder to see what's actually happening.

Worth the 20 minutes:
https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/who-cares-about-consumer-ai/id1680633614?i=1000766507145
```

**Why this moment passed the rubric:**
- Specificity: immediately makes you think of someone having a layoff conversation
- Novelty: reframes a story most people accepted at face value
- Actionability: gives language to push back in a meeting or thread
- Trust density: doesn't dismiss AI, just adds accuracy
- Channel fit: natural Slack or email forward in any team discussing workforce AI

## Card 2: Shopping Agents Might Be Solving the Wrong Problem
Format used: Product Question
Rubric scores: Specificity ✓ Novelty ✓ Actionability ✓ Trust density ✓ Channel fit ✓ (product team Slack, design review)
Target sender: Product designer, ecommerce strategist, retail founder, UX lead
Target recipient: A commerce team, marketplace operator, anyone building or evaluating agentic shopping features

```
AIDB Product Question — Who Cares About Consumer AI

The distinction:
Some shopping is purely task-based. Get the thing. Done.
But a lot of shopping is browsing — discovery, comparison,
preference formation. The quest is the point.

The question nobody is asking:
Are we building agents for the first type of shopping
and assuming they'll work for the second?

If agents remove the quest, they may also remove
part of what makes shopping satisfying.

https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/who-cares-about-consumer-ai/id1680633614?i=1000766507145
```

**Why this moment passed the rubric:**
- Specificity: immediately useful for anyone on a commerce or product team
- Novelty: separates two behaviors most agentic commerce discussion conflates
- Actionability: reframes a product decision before it gets made wrong
- Trust density: doesn't say agents are bad, just asks which problem they're solving
- Channel fit: natural product team discussion card, strongest in a design or strategy meeting

---

## Notes for future use

These two cards demonstrate different ends of the format range:
- Card 1 (Reality Check) has the broadest audience — anyone following AI news
- Card 2 (Product Question) has a narrower but higher-value audience — product and commerce teams

For most episodes, you will find one of each. The Reality Check travels further. The Product Question lands harder with the right person.

Build both when you can. Prioritize the one that better fits the episode's core argument.

## Project Overview

Arts of August is a Maine-based coastal art studio by artist Kaleigh Anderson. The brand includes original oil paintings, fine art prints, gold foil / gold leaf details, coastal scenes, commissions, and Maine- and Martha’s Vineyard-inspired artwork.

This project focused on improving the website’s visibility and extractability for generative search engines and AI answer engines, including ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Google AI Overviews, and Bing Copilot.

The goal was not just traditional SEO.

The goal was to make Arts of August easier for AI systems to understand, cite, summarize, and recommend.

## The Challenge

Small creative businesses often have beautiful work, loyal customers, and meaningful stories, but their websites do not always explain those signals clearly enough for search engines or AI systems.

For Arts of August, the substance was there:

- a real artist
- a recognizable coastal point of view
- original oil-on-canvas work
- gold foil and gold leaf process details
- Maine and Martha’s Vineyard place-based inspiration
- commissions
- art markets and showings
- Etsy listings
- customer reviews
- local credibility

But much of that context was either too light, too scattered, or not structured in a way that AI systems could confidently extract.

The project became a translation problem:

How do you make a creative business understandable to both humans and machines without flattening the art into keywords?

## Why GEO Matters for Creative Businesses

GEO — generative engine optimization — is the practice of making a brand, business, or website easier for AI-powered search and answer systems to understand.

Traditional SEO often focuses on rankings, keywords, backlinks, and page structure.

GEO adds another layer:

- Can an AI system understand who the artist is?
- Can it identify the studio as a real entity?
- Can it extract accurate facts about materials, process, products, locations, events, and commissions?
- Can it distinguish supported claims from vague marketing language?
- Can it cite the website as a useful source?
- Can it answer a customer’s question clearly using the site’s content?

For a creative business, this matters because customers are increasingly using AI tools to ask questions like:

- “Find Maine coastal artists who sell prints.”
- “Who paints Martha’s Vineyard-inspired artwork?”
- “Where can I buy original coastal oil paintings?”
- “What local artists are showing work near Portland, Maine?”
- “Can I commission a custom painting of a meaningful place?”

If the website does not clearly answer those questions, AI systems may skip over it, even if the business is a perfect match.

## Audit Findings

The Arts of August website already had a strong foundation.

Several important elements were already in place:

- `llms.txt` file at `/llms.txt`
- LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema on the homepage
- FAQPage JSON-LD schema
- FAQ section with clear Q&A content
- Quality Promise / trust-building language
- Artist bio and basic studio information
- Meta title, meta description, canonical tags, and Open Graph tags
- Crawlable site structure
- Existing `og:image`

The site was not starting from zero.

But the GEO audit identified several gaps that could limit AI visibility and citation quality:

- no Person schema for Kaleigh Anderson
- no WebSite schema
- no BreadcrumbList schema
- no Product JSON-LD for individual Etsy listings
- `llms.txt` was too thin and needed richer extractable studio facts
- About content was too light for AI citation
- materials and process details were not specific enough
- events and showings were not structured clearly
- testimonials and reviews were not yet prepared for trust signals or Review schema
- third-party corroboration opportunities needed to be identified

The opportunity was to strengthen Arts of August as an entity: artist, studio, location, materials, process, products, events, reviews, and third-party credibility.

## Technical GEO Improvements

The first layer of work focused on structured data and AI-readable site architecture.

The site now includes:

- Person schema for Kaleigh Anderson
- WebSite schema
- BreadcrumbList schema
- Article schema on the dedicated About page
- ImageObject schema
- Review schema tied to a real Etsy review
- Event schema for upcoming events and showings
- Product JSON-LD for every Etsy listing in `ShopEtsy.tsx`
- Product schema including name, image, URL, price, currency, brand, material, country of origin, and manufacturer details
- Expanded `llms.txt` with richer extractable facts about the studio, materials, shipping/returns, commissions, and contact details
- Page metadata, canonical tags, and sitemap entry for the new About page

We also preserved what was already working:

- existing LocalBusiness schema
- existing FAQPage schema
- existing Open Graph image
- crawlable page structure

The goal was not to over-engineer the site.

The goal was to give AI systems clearer facts in formats they already know how to read.

## Content & Entity Strategy

The second layer of work focused on content.

For AI search, structured data is helpful, but it is not enough. AI systems also need substantive prose that explains the business clearly and accurately.

We developed a content strategy focused on entity-building:

- who Kaleigh Anderson is
- what Arts of August is
- where the studio is rooted
- what materials are used
- how the paintings are created
- what places inspire the work
- what products are available
- what commissions are accepted
- where the work has been shown
- what customers have said

This content was designed to be useful for humans first, while also making the brand more extractable for AI systems.

## Materials & Process Content

We developed more specific language around materials and process.

Key facts prepared for the website included:

- primary medium: oil on canvas
- select works include gold foil or gold leaf
- gold foil is applied directly to the canvas and painted into the surface
- most works begin from photograph references
- the photograph becomes a starting point, not a copy
- Kaleigh interprets images through composition, color, lighting, layering, and brushwork
- original paintings often take around 30–40 hours, depending on size, detail, drying time, and materials
- varnish is part of the finishing process, but specific varnish naming should be confirmed before making a permanent materials claim

Gamblin Satin Varnish appeared in social content, but should not be stated globally unless confirmed.

Good GEO is not just about adding more claims. It is about making claims that are accurate, supportable, and safe to repeat.

## Product & Review Schema

Product visibility was another important part of the GEO strategy.

Because Arts of August sells through Etsy, the website needed to make product data easier to understand and connect back to live listings.

Product JSON-LD was added for individual Etsy listings, including:

- product name
- image
- URL
- price
- currency
- brand
- material
- country of origin
- manufacturer details

Product schema helps connect Etsy listings back to the Arts of August brand.

We also identified review and trust-building opportunities.

A real Etsy review for *Tethered by the Tide* was prepared as social proof. The review included a 5-star rating and praised color, detail, seller communication, shipping speed, and overall experience.

Review schema was added carefully using a real Etsy review.

Key review principles:

- no fabricated review data was used
- Review schema was only used where connected accurately to the product/review
- informal or family-related comments should not be turned into formal Review schema unless permission and accuracy are confirmed

Trust signals only work if they are true.

## Image and Alt Text Strategy

We created descriptive alt text guidance for artist, process, and product images.

Examples included:

- “Kaleigh Anderson painting outdoors on Martha’s Vineyard near the Aquinnah cliffs”
- “Oil painting of Lucy Vincent Beach in progress on an easel”
- “Arts of August coastal oil painting with gold foil detail”
- “Maine rowboat oil painting by Kaleigh Anderson”

The goal was not to stuff keywords into images.

The goal was to describe what the image actually shows in a way that supports accessibility, search, and AI understanding.
